Law on the Elections Commission

Introduction and Name

    (a) This is the law to determine the responsibilities, powers, scope of work, and the qualifications and code of conduct for the members of the "Elections Commission" established under the Constitution of the Republic of Maldives.

    (b) This law shall be cited as the "Elections Commission Act".

Legal Status

    The Elections Commission is an independent legal entity with the power to sue and be sued in its own name, to conduct transactions in its own name, and having its own seal.

Composition of the Commission

    The Elections Commission shall be composed of 5 (five) members.

Appointment of Members

    (a) Members of the Commission shall be appointed by the President of the Republic in accordance with this Article.

    (b) Whenever members or a member need to be appointed to the Commission, the President of the Republic shall submit to the People's Majlis a number of names no less than the number of members to be appointed.

    (c) The names submitted by the President of the Republic to the People's Majlis under subsection (b) of this Article shall be selected from among those who respond to a public announcement. When submitting these names, the names of other individuals who responded to the public announcement shall also be sent to the People's Majlis for its information.

    (d) From the names submitted by the President of the Republic to the People's Majlis under subsection (b) of this Article, the President of the Republic shall appoint those who are approved by a majority of the members present and voting in a session of the People's Majlis.

    (e) Members appointed to the Commission shall be individuals who possess the education and capability to fulfill the responsibilities and duties of the Commission, and who meet the criteria specified in Article 5 of this Act.

Qualifications for Members

    While serving as members of the Commission, members must fulfill the following criteria:

    (a) Be a Muslim;
    (b) Be a Maldivian citizen;
    (c) Be at least 25 years of age;
    (d) Not have been convicted of an offense for which a hadd is prescribed in Islam within the past 5 (five) years;
    (e) Not be holding an elected or appointed political post under the Constitution of the Republic of Maldives or a law;
    (f) Not be employed by the government or any other entity;
    (g) Not be serving a sentence longer than twelve months for a criminal offense, and if convicted of a criminal offense and sentenced to a term longer than twelve months, five years must have passed since the sentence was served or pardoned;
    (h) Not be a member of a political party or an active person in a political party.

Term of Members

    (a) The term of a member appointed to the Commission shall be one term of 5 (five) years from the date of appointment.

    (b) Notwithstanding subsection (a) of this Article, the term of any member, except a member dismissed under Article 14 of this Act, may be extended by the People's Majlis for a period not exceeding an additional 5 (five) years.

Appointment of the President and Vice President of the Commission

    (a) The President of the Republic shall appoint a President and a Vice President of the Commission from among its members in accordance with this Article.

    (b) Whenever a President or Vice President of the Commission needs to be appointed, the President of the Republic shall submit the name or names of a person or persons from among the members appointed to the Commission to the People's Majlis.

    (c) From the names submitted by the President of the Republic to the People's Majlis as per subsection (b) of this Article, the President of the Republic shall appoint the person approved by a majority of the members present and voting in a session of the People's Majlis to the post of President or Vice President of the Commission.

Responsibilities of the President of the Commission

    The responsibilities of the President of the Commission are stated below:

    (a) To provide advice and guidance necessary to carry out the Commission's responsibilities in a result-oriented and robust manner, in accordance with the Constitution and the law;
    (b) To make decisions related to the administration of the Commission, such as setting the agenda for the Commission's meetings and determining the date and time for meetings;
    (c) To set an example and establish leadership in adherence to ethical standards for the Commission, its members, and its staff;
    (d) In consultation with the Commission, to establish principles for conducting the various administrative tasks of the Commission, ensure that administrative matters are conducted in accordance with those principles, and take necessary measures;
    (e) To preside over the meetings of the Commission; and
    (f) To implement the decisions made by the Commission.

Vice President of the Commission

    In the event that the President of the Commission is unable to perform the duties of his office, or is on leave, or if the position becomes vacant, the Vice President of the Commission shall temporarily assume the responsibilities of his office. In addition, he shall also carry out the responsibilities assigned to him by the President of the Commission.

Vacancy of a Position

    (a) A position of a member of the Commission shall be deemed vacant in the following circumstances:
    (1) Upon completion of the term of membership;
    (2) Upon resignation;
    (3) Upon removal from the Commission's membership in accordance with Article 14 of this Act;
    (4) Upon contesting for a political post elected under the Constitution or a law;
    (5) Upon death;
    (6) Upon losing any of the qualifications required by this Act for members of the Commission.

    (b) The position of the President or Vice President of the Commission shall be deemed vacant upon the occurrence of a situation where a member's position is deemed vacant, or upon the resignation of either the President or the Vice President from their respective positions.

Appointment to Vacant Positions

    (a) In the event a Commission member's position becomes vacant due to the circumstance mentioned in Article 10 (a)(1) of this Act, the necessary consultation with the People's Majlis as per Article 4 of this Act must be sought and concluded 15 (fifteen) days before the vacancy occurs.

    (b) If the position of the President of the Commission, the Vice President of the Commission, or a member of the Commission becomes vacant due to any of the circumstances mentioned in Article 10(a)(2), (3), (4), (5), and (6) of this Act, a person shall be newly appointed to that position within 60 (sixty) days of the position becoming vacant.

    (c) In order to appoint members to the Commission upon the expiry of a term as per subsection (a) of this Article, the names proposed by the President of the Republic shall be submitted to the People's Majlis 60 (sixty) days before the term expires.

Responsibilities of Members

    The responsibilities of the members of the Commission are stated below:
    (a) To perform the responsibilities of a Commission member independently, justly, without partiality, and without yielding to any influence;
    (b) To diligently attend Commission meetings and actively participate in them in a collaborative manner;
    (c) To carry out the responsibilities assigned to the member from among the Commission's duties, and to provide advice and assistance to the President of the Commission in carrying out the Commission's responsibilities;
    (d) To uphold the principles of independence and integrity required of a Commission member;
    (e) To always act in good faith while working as a member of the Commission, and to prioritize the achievement of the Commission's objectives and the protection of its interests.

Assumption of Office by Members

    A member of the Commission may assume his office only after taking the following oath before a Judge and signing the written oath: "I, ......... (name of person) ......... swear by Almighty Allah to respect the religion of Islam, to uphold the Constitution of the Republic of Maldives, to be loyal to the Maldivian nation, and to remain steadfast in performing the duties and responsibilities of a member of the Elections Commission honestly and faithfully, in accordance with the Constitution and laws of the Republic of Maldives."

Removal from Membership

    A member of the Commission may be removed from office in a circumstance as stated in subsection (a) of this Article, and in the manner stated in subsection (b) of this Article.
    (a) If it is found that a member has acted contrary to the matters stated in Article 17 (a) and (b) of this Act, or if a situation arises that prevents them from performing their duties, or if it is deemed that they lack the capability to perform their duties, or if a situation stated in Article 17-9 (a) of this Act arises;
    (b) If the committee of the People's Majlis designated under its rules to oversee independent commissions and posts finds that a situation described in subsection (a) of this Article has occurred, the member shall be removed from membership upon a resolution passed by a majority of the members present and voting in a session of the People's Majlis.

Resignation

    (a) If a member of the Commission wishes to resign from their membership, they shall submit a letter stating this to the President of the Republic, and the member shall be removed from the Commission's membership upon receipt of the letter by the President of the Republic.

    (b) If the President or Vice President of the Commission wishes to resign from their position, they shall submit a letter stating this to the President of the Republic, and upon receipt of the letter by the President of the Republic, that person shall be removed from the position of President or Vice President of the Commission.

    (c) The resignation of the President or Vice President of the Commission from their position under subsection (b) of this Article shall not be considered as a resignation from their membership of the Commission.

Salary and Allowances

    (a) The salary and other allowances for the President, Vice President, and members of the Commission shall be determined by the People's Majlis.

    (b) The salary and allowances provided to the President and Vice President of the Commission shall not be reduced during their term of office. Similarly, the salary and allowances provided to the members of the Commission shall not be reduced during their term of membership.

    (c) The salary and allowances for the employees of the Commission shall be determined by the Commission in consultation with the Ministry of Finance and Treasury.

Working Independently Without Influence

    (a) In fulfilling the duties of the Commission, members of the Commission shall not yield to any fear or influence.

    (b) In deciding any matter of the Commission, or in conducting a matter, or in consultation, or in determining a policy, no personal interest shall be sought, either directly or indirectly.

    (c) All actions of the Commission must be carried out for the public interest, in a manner that achieves the Commission's objectives.

    (d) To ensure transparency, accurate information regarding a matter shall be provided to the public in an easily accessible manner at the time of the matter.

    (e) The Commission has full legal authority, independence, and power in conducting its work, organizing its administrative structure, carrying out its tasks, planning its programs, and taking necessary actions.

  (h) After leaving the membership of the Commission, a member shall not engage in any employment or work that conflicts with the interests they served as a member of the Commission, until a period of 12 (twelve) months has passed.

17-6. (a) In fulfilling their official responsibilities and duties, members of the Commission shall uphold the following standards:

(1) To abide by and respect all laws of the Maldives, and to obey the orders of a party issuing a lawful order in the performance of official duties.

(2) To make decisions without any personal bias and based solely on the evidence at hand, and in doing so, to be sincere, fair, and diligent in one's work and duties.

(11) Not to participate in political debates, not to use one's position to achieve a political objective, not to do anything that implies support for or opposition to political activities, and not to do anything that implies yielding to political influence.

(12) To constantly assess situations where Commission employees might be exposed to acts of corruption, and to shape their official responsibilities, work ethics, and the Commission's administrative procedures in a way that their actions do not pave the way for corruption.

(13) To implement necessary procedures to keep Commission members and employees away from acts of corruption, to provide them with education and training, to be vigilant about their financial situation to ensure they remain free from unjust enrichment, and for Commission members to set an example of being protected from corruption.

(14) If ordered, pressured, or forced to do or not do a specific act in violation of the code of conduct, whether a member or an employee of the Commission, or if improper influence is exerted that obstructs the employee's work from being carried out with integrity, to promptly report the matter to the Prosecutor General, the Anti-Corruption Commission, and the People's Majlis.

(15) Not to do anything improper towards any person who submits a matter to the Commission.

(b) The responsibilities stated in subsection (a) of this Article shall be considered a fundamental principle of employment for the President, members, and employees of the Commission. This applies even if there is no employment agreement, or if any of these matters are not included in an existing employment agreement. And, a violation of any of these matters shall be considered to have given rise to a situation for taking disciplinary action against that person.

Accountability of the Commission

17-11. And they shall cooperate positively in any matter that the People's Majlis needs to investigate and review regarding the functioning of the Commission or any work being done by the Commission.

Protection

    No civil or criminal proceedings shall be instituted against the President, Vice President, a member, or an employee of the Commission for any act done or omitted in good faith in their official capacity, in the course of discharging the duties and responsibilities of the Commission and in exercising the powers vested in the Commission, in accordance with the law or within the scope of a power granted by law.

Commission Meetings

    A meeting of the Commission shall be held at least once every month.

Quorum and Voting

    (a) A meeting of the Commission may be held only when more than half of the members of the Commission are present.
    (b) Any matter shall be decided by the Commission only with the majority of the members present and voting at the meeting.
    (c) No member shall participate to any extent in deciding a matter in which they have a personal interest, stake, or financial benefit. And upon knowing of such a matter, they shall inform the other members of the Commission and abstain from making any decision related to that matter.

Maintaining Records of Meetings

20-1. (a) The minutes of the Commission's meetings shall be written verbatim and maintained. And audio recordings of those meetings shall be maintained.
(b) The minutes shall be written and prepared without omitting or adding to the statements made by the members, in a way that a statement made by a Commission member is reflected in the minutes as it was said, and in a manner that clearly shows how the proceedings of the meeting took place.
(c) If the Commission conducts any investigation or takes any testimony, it shall be minuted verbatim. And an audio recording of that investigation shall be maintained.
(d) The minutes of the Commission's meetings shall include details of the attendance of the Commission members and how they voted.
(e) The attendance of the Commission's meetings shall be published on the Commission's website once every month.
(f) Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in any other law, the documents and recordings mentioned in subsections (a) and (c) of this Article shall be safely maintained by the Commission for a period of at least 10 (ten) years.

Responsibilities and Powers of the Commission

    The responsibilities and powers of the Commission are stated below.
    (a) To undertake, conduct, supervise, and facilitate matters required for elections and public referendums, and to ensure that elections and public referendums are conducted fairly, without fear, intimidation, violence, undue influence, and corruption, in a manner that allows citizens to fully exercise their right to vote with freedom and fairness;
    (b) To prepare the register of persons eligible to vote in elections and public referendums, to make necessary amendments to and maintain that register, and to make all necessary arrangements regarding the holding of elections and public referendums;
    (c) To hold elections and public referendums within the period specified by law and to announce the results of those votes within the period specified by law;
    (d) To compile registers of persons eligible to vote in electoral constituencies, to make necessary amendments to those registers for the period specified by law, and to continue publishing them in the government gazette in accordance with the law;
    (e) To determine, demarcate, alter, and review the names and boundaries of electoral constituencies for each election based on principles established by law, and to continue publishing these changes in the government gazette;
    (f) To register political parties and to perform duties related to political parties as determined by law for the Commission;
    (g) To provide information and raise public awareness regarding the conduct of elections and the purpose of elections;
    (h) To establish rules for elections and public referendums that the Commission is required to conduct and supervise by the Constitution and law;
    (i) To train the staff of the Commission and parties to whom the Commission assigns work;
    (j) To provide materials and resources used in elections and to plan and manage related matters;
    (k) To accredit international and Maldivian observers who are active in monitoring the conduct of elections;
    (l) To determine the locations for placing ballot boxes in elections and public referendums; and if the head of a location determined by the Commission or the party in charge of it does not provide the assistance and cooperation requested by the Commission for placing ballot boxes and conducting the voting process, to take all necessary actions for the Commission to fulfill its responsibilities after giving reasonable notice to that party;
    (m) To establish and arrange for citizens eligible to vote who are living outside the Maldives to vote in elections and public referendums;
    (n) Upon the conclusion of each election or public referendum conducted and supervised by the Commission, to publish a report of that election or public referendum in accordance with the law;
    (o) To carry out all tasks stated in the Constitution and election-related laws as required to be done by the Commission.

Body to Conduct and Supervise Elections

    (a) Elections and public referendums which are determined by the Constitution and law to be conducted and supervised by the Commission shall be conducted and supervised by the Commission and by parties to whom the Commission assigns the work.
    (b) In order to fulfill the responsibilities of the Commission and to implement the powers vested in it under this Act, the Commission is empowered to establish offices in administrative and electoral constituencies and to employ temporary and permanent staff required for such offices.

Elections to be Supervised by the Commission

    (a) The elections conducted and supervised by the Commission are stated below:
    (1) Presidential election;
    (2) People's Majlis election;
    (3) Elections for councils established for the decentralized administration of administrative divisions;
    (4) Holding public referendums required by the People's Majlis or the President of the Republic.

    (b) The Commission shall conduct and supervise the by-elections for the elections stated in subsections (a)(2) and (a)(3) of this Article.

    (c) In addition to the elections stated in subsection (a) of this Article, the Commission shall conduct and supervise elections that are determined by the Constitution of the Republic of Maldives and other laws to be supervised and conducted by the Commission.

Voting and Counting

    (a) A secret ballot shall be cast in elections and public referendums held under the supervision of the Commission.

    (b) Upon the conclusion of each vote, in every polling station, the person to whom the Commission has entrusted that station shall, in the presence of the contesting candidates or persons appointed by the candidates if they are present, and in the presence of any other party permitted by law to observe the counting of the vote, count the votes of that station, record the number of votes received by each candidate or the number of votes received for the question posed in a public referendum, and announce the result of the vote taken at that station at that station.

Election-related Complaints

    Any party that has a complaint regarding a decision of the Commission concerning any election or public referendum, or an election result, or a matter that they believe has occurred in violation of the law and regulations in an election-related matter, may submit that complaint to the High Court of the Maldives to seek a decision.

Appointment of Secretary-General and Staff

    (a) The Commission shall appoint a Secretary-General for its administrative management. The Secretary-General shall manage the Commission's administration in accordance with the regulations made by the Commission.

    (b) The Commission shall employ the staff required by the Commission and dismiss staff from employment.

Financial Matters

    (a) The funds necessary for the Commission to fully discharge its responsibilities shall be released to the Commission each year from the state treasury under a budget approved by the People's Majlis. For this purpose, the Commission shall, in consultation with the Ministry of Finance and Treasury, prepare its budget and submit it to the People's Majlis each year along with the state budget.

    (b) Any financial aid given to the Commission by any person, association, or foreign government shall not be used for any purpose other than to carry out the matters within the responsibilities of the Commission.

    (c) An account of the Commission's income, expenditure, assets, and financial liabilities shall be written and maintained in accordance with regulations made by the Commission in consultation with the Auditor General, and after auditing, a financial statement shall be sent to the President of the Republic and the People's Majlis along with the annual report mentioned in Article 28 of this Act.

Annual Report

    (a) An annual report compiled on the activities of the Commission shall be sent by the Commission to the President of the Republic and the People's Majlis before the 28th of February each year.

    (b) The annual report mentioned in subsection (a) of this Article shall include the following matters that occurred during the reporting period:
    (1) Election-related information;
    (2) Information on election-related complaints and how those complaints were handled;
    (3) Accounts of election-related expenditure;
    (4) Major changes to the register of eligible voters;
    (5) Information regarding changes made to the names and boundaries of electoral constituencies;
    (6) The financial statement mentioned in Article 27(n) of this Act;
    (7) Details of administrative matters that occurred during the year regarding the Commission's administration and staff;
    (8) Changes to the political party register;
    (9) And information regarding other important work done by the Commission.

    (c) The Commission shall publicly publish the report mentioned in subsection (a) of this Article within 14 (fourteen) days of sending it to the President of the Republic and the People's Majlis.

    (d) Notwithstanding subsection (a) of this Article, in a special situation, if the Commission deems it necessary, it has the discretion to submit a special report regarding that situation to the President of the Republic and the People's Majlis.

Formation of the Commission and Submission of Member Nominees

    (a) A Commission shall be formed and its members, President, and Vice President shall be appointed in accordance with this Act within 60 (sixty) days of the first session of the first People's Majlis elected under Article 296 of the Constitution.

    (b) In order to act in accordance with subsection (a) of this Article:
    (1) The names proposed by the President of the Republic to be included in the Commission shall be sent to the People's Majlis within 20 (twenty) days from the date of the first session of the first People's Majlis elected under Article 296(b) of the Constitution.
    (2) The Majlis shall make a decision and inform the President of the Republic within 20 (twenty) days from the date the President of the Republic submits a name or names to the People's Majlis in accordance with subsections (b)(1) and (b)(3) of this Article.
    (3) If the Majlis does not approve a name submitted by the President of the Republic to the People's Majlis in accordance with this Article, the President of the Republic shall submit a new name or names to the Majlis within 5 (five) days from the date the President of the Republic is informed of this.

Elections Commission Appointed During the Transitional Period

    (a) The Elections Commission appointed in accordance with Article 276 of the Constitution shall have all the responsibilities and powers of the Commission appointed under this Act. And from the day this Act comes into force, the other articles of this Act, except for Articles 4, 6, and 29, shall apply to the Elections Commission established in accordance with Article 276 of the Constitution.

    (b) The Elections Commission established under subsection (a) of this Article shall continue to exist until an Elections Commission is established in accordance with Article 29 of this Act.

    (c) If a position of a member of the Elections Commission established in accordance with subsection (a) of this Article becomes vacant, members shall be appointed to that Commission in accordance with Article 277 of the Constitution.

Date of Commencement of the Act

    This Act shall come into force on the date it is passed, ratified, and published in the Government Gazette of the Maldives.

Making and Enforcing Regulations

    (a) The regulations required to be made under this Act shall be made and enforced by the Commission. These regulations shall be made and finalized within 06 (six) months of the formation of the Commission in accordance with Article 29(a) of this Act.

    (b) Subsection (a) of this Article shall not prevent the Commission from amending a regulation made under subsection (a) or from making an additional regulation under this Act after the period mentioned in that subsection if the Commission deems it necessary.

    (c) Notwithstanding subsection (a) of this Article, subsection (a) shall not prevent the Elections Commission established under Article 276 of the Constitution from making a regulation necessary for it to perform its duties and responsibilities.

Definitions

    In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires;
    "Auditor General" refers to the Auditor General appointed under the Audit Act (Law No. 4/2007).
    "Commission" refers to the Elections Commission.
