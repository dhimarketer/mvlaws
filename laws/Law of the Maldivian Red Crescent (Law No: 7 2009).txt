Law of the Maldivian Red Crescent (Law No: 7/2009)

An unofficial translation of the Dhivehi text provided is as follows:

1. Introduction and Name

(a) This is the law that establishes an independent, voluntary organization named the Maldivian Red Crescent to work on and provide assistance in humanitarian matters, grants it legal status to operate in the Maldives, and outlines its fundamental principles.[1]

(b) This law shall be cited as the "Law of the Maldivian Red Crescent."[1]

2. Legal Status

(a) The Maldivian Red Crescent is an independent legal entity with its own seal, possessing the power to sue and be sued, and to enter into transactions in its own name.[1]

(b) The Maldivian Red Crescent is an independent voluntary aid society. In cooperation with the International Federation of the Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies, the Maldivian Red Crescent is the sole national society authorized to operate within the territory of the Maldives to achieve the objectives stated in Article 3 of this law.[1]

(c) To achieve its objectives, the Maldivian Red Crescent is empowered and authorized to conduct its activities within the territory of the Maldives in accordance with the laws of the Maldives.[1]

(d) When conducting its activities as stipulated in this law, the Maldivian Red Crescent shall, as far as possible, collaborate with and support other state institutions active in humanitarian affairs and emergencies.[1]

3. Objectives of the Maldivian Red Crescent

(a) The primary objective of the Maldivian Red Crescent is to provide humanitarian assistance to those in distress and to rescue and protect them from such situations.[1]

(b) To achieve the primary objective stated in subsection (a) of this article, the main objectives of the Maldivian Red Crescent are:

    To provide humanitarian assistance to military and civilian persons who are in distress or have suffered harm during times of war, unrest, and peace.[1]

    To protect the dignity and physical integrity of persons in distress or who have suffered harm, while providing aid during times of war, unrest, and peace.[1]

    To provide assistance to those affected during epidemics, situations requiring disease prevention measures, and other emergencies that pose a threat to humanity, and to work towards improving their conditions.[1]

    To cooperate with relevant state institutions when they are providing assistance to those who have suffered loss or been affected by various disasters and natural incidents.[1]

(c) In working to achieve the objectives stated in subsections (a) and (b) of this article, the Maldivian Red Crescent shall act with equality and without any adverse distinction based on race, country of origin, nationality, color, sex, age, physical or mental disability, religion, political or other opinion, financial capacity, family, island of origin, or any other similar criteria.

4. Fundamental Principles for the Activities of the Maldivian Red Crescent

(a) The activities conducted by the Maldivian Red Crescent to achieve its objectives under this law must be carried out in a manner that does not conflict with the Constitution and laws of the Maldives, and is in accordance with the fundamental principles of the International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement, the 1949 Geneva Conventions, and their Additional Protocols.

(b) In accordance with subsection (a) of this article, the Maldivian Red Crescent must uphold the principles and fulfill the responsibilities that members of the International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement and the International Federation of the Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies are required to adhere to.

5. Statutes and Rules of Procedure

In order to operate the Maldivian Red Crescent in accordance with this law, a Statute of the Maldivian Red Crescent shall be established. This Statute must define the following matters or the principles related to them:

(a) A General Assembly and a Governing Board to carry out the responsibilities of the Maldivian Red Crescent.

(b) Matters concerning the members of the Maldivian Red Crescent.

(c) The method of electing or appointing members to various posts within the Maldivian Red Crescent, the manner in which the responsibilities of these posts are assigned, and the term for which these responsibilities are assigned.

(d) The general meetings of the Maldivian Red Crescent, the conduct of these meetings, the presiding over these meetings, and the recording and maintenance of the minutes of these meetings.

(e) The method of voting in the general meetings and other meetings of the Maldivian Red Crescent, and the manner in which matters are passed or decisions are made in these meetings.

(f) The manner in which amendments or revisions can be made to the Statute of the Maldivian Red Crescent.[1]

(g) The manner in which the activities of the Maldivian Red Crescent shall be concluded, the procedure to be followed if the Maldivian Red Crescent has to be dissolved, and the procedure for dealing with the funds, property, and assets belonging to the society upon its dissolution.[1]

(h) The Rules of Procedure of the Maldivian Red Crescent, which shall provide further details on the matters stated above, the code of conduct to be followed by the members, elected or appointed members to various posts, employees, and delegates of the Maldivian Red Crescent, and the administrative, personnel, and financial affairs of the Maldivian Red Crescent.

6. Members

Individuals and legal entities may become members of the Maldivian Red Crescent in accordance with its Statute, without any discrimination based on nationality, race, sex, religion, language, social status, or political opinion.[2]

7. Income and Property

(a) The income, property, and assets received by the Maldivian Red Crescent shall be used solely for the purpose of achieving the objectives of the Maldivian Red Crescent. Under no circumstances shall any such asset be given, directly or indirectly, as a profit or bonus to the members of the Maldivian Red Crescent.

(b) Money and items given as wages or benefits to an official, employee, or member of the Maldivian Red Crescent for work done for the society, in accordance with the Statute or Rules of Procedure of the Maldivian Red Crescent, are exempted from subsection (a) of this article.

8. Use of the Emblem and Seal of the Maldivian Red Crescent

(a) The emblem and seal of the Maldivian Red Crescent shall be as described in the schedule of this law.

(b) The emblem or seal of the Maldivian Red Crescent shall not be used except by the Maldivian Red Crescent or by a party authorized by the Maldivian Red Crescent.

(c) A relevant court in the Maldives has the power to impose a fine not exceeding MVR 12,000 (twelve thousand) or to sentence to imprisonment for a term not exceeding 6 (six) months, any person who violates subsection (b) of this article.[1]

9. Dealings with the Government

(a) In dealings between the Maldivian Red Crescent and the government, the government shall primarily interact with the Maldivian Red Crescent through a ministry designated for this purpose by the President of the Republic.[1]

(b) Government institutions shall respect the activities conducted and the principles and responsibilities upheld by the Maldivian Red Crescent as stated in Article 4 of this law, and shall provide assistance to the fullest extent possible.

10. Establishment of the Maldivian Red Crescent and the Body Empowered and Responsible for Managing and Overseeing the Establishment Process

(a) The Maldivian Red Crescent shall be established and finalized within 6 (six) months from the date this law comes into effect, in accordance with this law and the Statute of the Maldivian Red Crescent.[1]

(b) The Interim Planning Group shall carry out, manage, and oversee all necessary actions to establish and finalize the Maldivian Red Crescent as stated in subsection (a) of this article. The Interim Planning Group shall be dissolved upon the establishment of the Maldivian Red Crescent in accordance with this law and the holding of its first general meeting.

11. Making and Implementing Regulations

(a) The Maldivian Red Crescent shall make and implement its Statute and Rules of Procedure in order to operate the Maldivian Red Crescent in accordance with this law.

(b) Notwithstanding subsection (a) of this article, subsection (a) does not prevent the Interim Planning Group from making and implementing the regulations and principles necessary to establish and finalize the Maldivian Red Crescent within the period specified in subsection (a) of Article 10 of this law.

12. Commencement of the Law

This law shall come into effect on the date of its ratification and publication in the Government Gazette.

13. Definitions of Words and Phrases

(a) In this law, unless expressly stated otherwise:

"Territory of the Maldives" refers to the territory of the Maldives as defined in the Constitution of the Republic of Maldives.

"Interim Planning Group" refers to the working group formed in 2006 under the name "Interim Planning Group," comprising representatives from relevant government authorities, the general public, and the International Federation of the Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies, with the objective of establishing and coordinating the work of a voluntary humanitarian aid organization in the name of the Maldivian Red Crescent in the Maldives.

(b) In this law, unless expressly stated or understood otherwise, words or phrases in the singular shall include the plural and words or phrases in the plural shall include the singular.

Schedule
The Emblem and Seal of the Maldivian Red Crescent

The emblem of the Maldivian Red Crescent is a red crescent, open to the right, on a white background. The seal of the Maldivian Red Crescent is a red crescent within two black concentric circles on a white background.
